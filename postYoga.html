8 Limbs of Yoga

I first learned about the 8 Limbs of Yoga in my 200hr Yoga Teacher Training through the Yoga & Ayurveda Center. I connected with the principle immediately. I am a member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints. The Yama’s and Niyama’s are in line with many of the teachings of Jesus Christ. I have been working to live by these principles all of my life. Aparigraha and Ishwara Pranidhana are particularly meaningful to me. They are clear principles that outline how to live a faith filled life.

I connect Aparigraha to putting your trust in God and believing that all things will work together for my own good. I connect Ishwara Pranidhana with aligning our will with the will of God. While these principles are similar, Aparigraha is necessary because we will never fully understand the mind of God in this life and while we will still have our own hopes and dreams we should strive to trust in whatever the end result may be. Ishwara Pranidhana is a higher level of living. It’s complete oneness with God. This is a lifelong pursuit. But I hope someday to follow the example of Jesus Christ and be able to say with sincerity “Not my will, but thine be done.”

In September 1995 a living prophet and 12 apostles released “The Family A Proclamation to the World” In this proclamation it says

“Happiness in family life is most likely to be achieved when founded upon the teachings of the Lord Jesus Christ. Successful marriages and families are established and maintained on principles of faith, prayer, repentance, forgiveness, respect, love, compassion, work, and wholesome recreational activities.”

I have actively striven to incorporate these principles in my family life since The Family Proclamation was released. The 8 Limbs of Yoga give further knowledge on how to apply these principles in my thoughts and actions.

Although the 8 Limbs of Yoga and my religious beliefs are similar, yoga is not a religion. I don't believe religion should be taught in Yoga classes or any other forms of public or group recreational activities. However, the 8 Limbs of Yoga are crucial to acknowledging the foundation of Yoga and many meditation practices. I plan to credit Patanjali and reference the Yoga Sutras once during each class
